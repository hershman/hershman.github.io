Regional vulnerability

Killeen ISD (Bell Co.) posts 25.56 %—exceptionally high for a large public district. Together with Loop ISD and several small CISDs over 15 %, this marks Bell, Gaines, Montague, and Montgomery counties as priority surveillance targets.

Policy implications

  1. Target the tail. Roughly 150 high‑exemption schools account for most residual risk; focused outreach would yield the greatest marginal gain.
  2. Engage NAUMS & similar hybrids. Three of the five worst performers share this model.
  3. Leverage Catholic / large urban examples. Many report 0 % exemptions, showing institutional policy can suppress exemption uptake.
  4. Monitor military‑adjacent districts. Killeen ISD’s spike may reflect transient populations and record transfer gaps.

Bottom line

Texas K‑12 exemption rates remain generally low but extremely heterogeneous. A small cluster of private hybrid schools and a handful of rural districts create a disproportionate share of vulnerability; precision outreach to those campuses would materially raise the state’s immunization floor without new statewide mandates.
